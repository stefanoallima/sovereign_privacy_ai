What actually happens to your data

Three things cloud AI
companies don’t lead with

People already use cloud AI for the most personal questions of their lives — not because they’ve assessed the risk, but because it’s fast and it’s there. These are the facts worth knowing first.

01

Your data trains their models

When you describe a health symptom, share a family situation, or ask about your finances, that text can become training material. Researchers at Google DeepMind demonstrated that prompts fed into large language models can be extracted verbatim by other users — including personal data, private correspondence, and sensitive details people thought they were asking in confidence.

📄 Extracting Training Data from Large Language Models — Carlini et al., Google DeepMind (2021). Training data including personal information was extracted verbatim.
⚖ New York Times v. OpenAI — NYT complaint (Dec 2023). Lawsuit alleged GPT-4 reproduces articles nearly verbatim, raising direct questions about what else was memorized.
02

Your prompts are product research

Every question you ask tells a cloud AI company something about you: what you worry about, what you’re going through, what you need. OpenAI explicitly reserves the right to use your interactions. That means the most personal questions of your life — health, money, relationships — become data about who you are and what you might buy.

📄 OpenAI Terms of Use — openai.com. “We may use Content to provide, maintain, develop, and improve our Services.”
📰 “OpenAI Is Eating the Startup Ecosystem” — The Atlantic (2024). Startup founders describe how OpenAI built competing features after observing how developers used their platform.
03

Free AI has a business model. You’re in it.

The fastest path from “free AI” to revenue is advertising — and the most valuable kind targets you based on what you’ve confided. You asked about a health scare. Now an ad knows you’re worried. OpenAI has discussed adding advertising. Microsoft already added ads to Copilot. Your most personal questions are exactly what makes that targeting precise.

📰 “OpenAI Considers Adding Advertising to Its Business” — The Wall Street Journal (2024).
📰 “Microsoft Adds Ads to Copilot” — The Verge (2024). Copilot now serves sponsored results inside AI-generated responses.

Sovereign AI was built on one principle: what you type stays on your machine. No training. No ads. No one else’s roadmap funded by your questions.

The Privacy Guard

Never Send a Secret
by Accident

Your sovereign council runs locally. When you need a more powerful AI model that would be impractical to run on your hardware, the Privacy Guard ensures your secrets are redacted before anything leaves your machine.

Local PII Detection

Your local AI automatically scans documents for names, addresses, financial data, and sensitive IDs. Everything detected stays on your device.

The Redaction Dashboard

When a council member needs a second opinion from a powerful cloud model, the app alerts you first. You get a side-by-side view to redact or approve specific data points before they ever leave your machine.

You Control the Leak

The cloud gets the context; you keep the identity. Only anonymized categorical attributes (income bracket, employment type) reach the network. Real values are filled back in locally.

Incognito Mode

Disappearing chats that leave zero trace. Messages exist only in memory — never written to disk, never persisted. Close the conversation and it's gone forever.

How It Works

The Privacy Pipeline

Your message goes through multiple privacy layers before any data reaches the cloud.

1
Your Message "I earn €62,500 and have a mortgage at Keizersgracht 42..."
↓
2
Local PII Detection Built-in Qwen3-8B identifies: name, address, BSN, salary figures
↓
3
You Review & Approve Redaction dashboard shows exactly what will be masked. You decide.
↓
4

↓
5
Re-hydration (Local) Cloud response with [INCOME] placeholders filled with your real values locally
EU-Grade Security

High-Performance,
Zero-Retention

Need a more powerful model than what runs locally? Configure any OpenAI-compatible API endpoint. We default to EU-based Nebius (Finland & France) — but you must enable Zero Data Retention (ZDR) in your Nebius account settings for maximum privacy.

ChaCha20-Poly1305

Military-grade encryption for all sensitive data stored locally. Keys in your system credential manager, not in files.

Configurable Cloud Endpoint

When you need a more powerful model, connect any OpenAI-compatible API. We default to EU-based Nebius — enable ZDR in your Nebius settings so your data is never stored.

Embedded AI Engine

Built-in Qwen3-8B runs entirely on your hardware. No separate installs, no background services, no external dependencies.

Windows Credential Store

Encryption keys stored in your OS credential manager. Not in config files, not in environment variables.
