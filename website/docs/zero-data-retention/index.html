Zero Data Retention

What it means, why it matters, and how to enable it.

What is zero data retention?

By default, most AI cloud providers log your API requests and may use them to improve their models. Zero data retention (ZDR) is a commitment from the provider that your requests are not stored after the response is returned — no logs, no training data, no retention.

⚠️ Not enabled by default Zero data retention is a separate account setting that must be explicitly requested or activated. Simply having an API key does not guarantee your data is not being stored.

Why it matters for Smart Shield mode

In Sovereign AI's Smart Shield mode, your personal details are replaced with safe placeholders before anything reaches the cloud (e.g. your email becomes [EMAIL]). This already protects your raw PII.

However, the prompt itself — your question and its context — is still sent to the cloud provider. Without zero data retention, that provider could store and process this sanitised prompt. Enabling ZDR means the provider commits that nothing is retained after the response is returned.

✓ Best practice Smart Shield + Zero Data Retention = your raw PII never leaves your device, and the sanitised prompt is not retained by the cloud provider. This is the recommended configuration.

How to enable zero data retention on Nebius AI Studio Required

Sovereign AI recommends Nebius AI Studio for Smart Shield mode because it offers zero data retention on request. To enable it:

  1. Go to studio.nebius.com and log in to your account.
  2. Open Settings → Privacy & Data (or contact Nebius support at support@nebius.com) to request zero data retention for your workspace.
  3. Once confirmed, your API requests will not be logged or used for model training.
ℹ️ Enterprise accounts Zero data retention is typically available on paid or enterprise plans. Check Nebius's current terms at nebius.com/legal for the latest information.

Other providers

Sovereign AI works with any OpenAI-compatible endpoint. If you use a different provider, look for equivalent settings labelled:

  • "Zero data retention" or "ZDR"
  • "No training on API data"
  • "Data processing agreement" (DPA) with no-logs clause
  • "Enterprise privacy mode"

Providers such as OpenAI offer ZDR as a paid enterprise feature. Always verify the current policy directly with your provider.

Sovereign AI without any cloud provider

If you prefer not to rely on cloud providers at all, select Local Only mode during setup. All inference runs on your device using the embedded Qwen3 model. Zero data retention is not a concern because nothing leaves your machine.
